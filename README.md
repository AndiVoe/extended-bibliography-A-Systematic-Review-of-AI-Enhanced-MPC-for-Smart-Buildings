# Extended Bibliography — A Systematic Review of AI-Enhanced MPC for Smart Buildings

## Overview

This repository contains an extended bibliography compiled in support of a systematic review on **Artificial Intelligence (AI)-Enhanced Model Predictive Control (MPC) for Smart Buildings**. The bibliography collects and organises peer-reviewed journal articles, conference papers, and preprints that document the current state of the art in applying AI and data-driven methods to building energy management and control.

## Scope

The bibliography covers research at the intersection of:

- **Model Predictive Control (MPC)** — white-box, grey-box, and black-box predictive controllers applied to building HVAC, heating, cooling, and energy storage systems
- **Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning** — neural networks, deep learning, reinforcement learning (RL), and hybrid AI approaches used to build, augment, or replace conventional MPC models
- **Smart Buildings & Energy Systems** — residential, commercial, and institutional buildings with grid-interactive or demand-flexible energy management
- **Field Implementations & Case Studies** — real-world deployments, living-lab experiments, and performance evaluations of advanced control strategies

### Key Topics

| Topic Area | Examples |
|---|---|
| Data-driven & hybrid building models | ANN-based MPC, grey-box models, physics-informed neural networks |
| Reinforcement Learning for HVAC | Deep RL, multi-agent RL, reward function design |
| Demand response & energy flexibility | Load shifting, grid-interactive buildings, demand-side management |
| Occupant comfort & behaviour | Thermal preference models, occupancy estimation, IEQ |
| Digital Twins | Building simulation, real-time monitoring, online model updating |
| Multi-agent systems | Distributed control, cooperative and competitive MAS |
| Energy storage & renewables | Thermal mass activation, heat pumps, PV integration |
| Review articles | Surveys on MPC, RL, AI in buildings, and smart-building control strategies |

## Bibliography Format

References in [`References.md`](References.md) follow **Zotero/Chicago author–date** style and are sorted alphabetically by first author. Each entry includes:

- Full author list, title, journal/venue, year, and volume/issue
- A citable DOI link for direct access to the source

## Intended Use

This bibliography is intended to:

1. Support reproducibility and transparency of the associated systematic review
2. Serve as a starting point for researchers entering the field of AI-enhanced building control
3. Provide a curated reading list covering both foundational theory and recent applied work (2013–2026)
